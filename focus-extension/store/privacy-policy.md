# LUMNA Focus — Privacy Policy

_Last updated: 2026-06-24_

LUMNA Focus is a browser extension that blocks websites you choose, to help you
stay focused. This policy explains what it handles and why.

## What the extension stores
- **Your connection token**, saved in the browser's local extension storage
  (`chrome.storage.local`). It identifies your block list on lumna.co. It never
  leaves your device except to talk to lumna.co (below).
- **A small status cache** (whether blocking is on, how many sites, last sync
  time), stored locally so the popup can display it.

## What it sends, and where
- The extension contacts **lumna.co only**, sending your connection token to
  read and update your block list (the websites you chose and the on/off state).
- Nothing is sent to any other server. There is no analytics or tracking.

## Access to your tabs
- The extension reads open tab URLs **locally**, only to reload tabs that are on
  a blocked site (so blocking takes effect immediately) and to return them when
  you turn blocking off. These URLs are **not transmitted anywhere**.

## What it does NOT do
- It does not collect personal information, browsing history, or analytics.
- It does not sell or share any data with third parties.
- It does not run remote code; blocking rules are simple data, not scripts.

## Data retention and deletion
- Your block list lives in your LUMNA account (or anonymously, keyed to a random
  token) on lumna.co. Remove the extension to stop all local storage and access.
  You can rotate or revoke your token from lumna.co/focus.
